Challenge

Setup fatigue was turning excited buyers into churned customers.

LG identified a significant friction point in the post-purchase journey: new owners were often overwhelmed by fragmented support resources — ranging from static manuals to external tutorials — leading to "setup fatigue" that increased churn risk and customer support overhead.

The objective was to engineer a scalable, digital-first solution that consolidated product education into a high-engagement, self-service ecosystem.

Approach

From text-heavy docs to immersive simulation.

I spearheaded the vision and execution of the webOS TV Simulator — a first-of-its-kind interactive platform designed to bridge the gap between purchase and product mastery. The strategy focused on three key pillars:

Centralized Product Intelligence
Unifying fragmented education into a single, interactive destination — no more scattered manuals or external searches.
Immersive Exploration
Enabling users to interact with simulated interfaces to build product confidence before and after purchase.
Visual-First Onboarding
Replacing traditional, high-friction support documents with guided digital tools that meet users where they are.
Action

End-to-end program leadership — from roadmap to global deployment.

As Program Lead, I orchestrated the full roadmap, managing a complex network of vendors, creative agencies, and internal technical stakeholders.

  • Data-Driven Journey Mapping — Defined the onboarding roadmap by synthesizing insights from Google Analytics, SEO search demand, and call-center drivers to prioritize the highest-impact modules first.
  • Accessibility & Inclusive Design — Led the accessibility review for the webOS TV Simulator to ensure strict alignment with WCAG 2.2 Level AA standards. Key initiatives included:
    • Keyboard-First Navigation — Implemented dynamic tabIndex management across interactive emulator components, enabling full functionality without a mouse.
    • Screen Reader Compatibility — Oversaw aria-label, role, and descriptive attributes for all non-semantic interactive elements.
    • Visual Accessibility — Validated color contrast and visual accessibility to support users with impairments; conducted manual QA testing to supplement automated tools.
  • Module Development — Directed the UI/UX strategy for three core components across the full build lifecycle.
  • Cross-Functional Orchestration — Managed Agile/Scrum workflows across UX, Legal, SEO, Product and Engineering teams to ensure accessibility standards and seamless Salesforce and Attentive integration.

Results

A reactive cost center transformed into a proactive engagement engine.

The simulator successfully transitioned LG's support infrastructure from reactive to proactive — customers were resolving issues independently, confidently, and on their own terms.

  • Enhanced User Autonomy — Customers demonstrated a significant shift toward self-service resolution, reducing the need for direct support intervention, evidenced by an 85–90% issue resolution rate.
  • Positive Sentiment — Feedback indicated improved customer confidence during the early ownership phase and higher satisfaction with the digital-first onboarding model, with +28% month-over-month engagement growth.
  • Lighthouse Excellence — Achieved a 100 accessibility score in Google Lighthouse audits and successfully aligned with WCAG 2.2 AA standards through internal and third-party audits, ensuring the tool was inclusive for a diverse global customer base.
  • Omnichannel Deployment — Successfully integrated across product pages, support sites, and customer portals.

Due to NDA restrictions, some data points and internal systems have been omitted or generalized. Schedule e-Coffee chat.

Key Learning

Interactive, visual experiences significantly outperform traditional documentation in driving product mastery and reducing support friction. By engineering guided digital tools and prioritizing WCAG 2.2 AA compliance, organizations can protect their NPS and retention by empowering all users at the most critical stage of the lifecycle: the first 30 days.
