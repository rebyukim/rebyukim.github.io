Challenge

Zero infrastructure. Zero playbook. Just a blank slate.

Onnuri English Ministry had no existing social media presence, no content workflows, and no creative team. The organization wanted to build a media program from the ground up — one that could authentically represent a vibrant community, grow an audience, and sustain itself beyond any single person's involvement.

The constraints were real: no budget for external vendors, volunteer contributors with varying skill levels, and a community that needed to see itself reflected in the content, not just marketed to.

Approach

Build the system first. Content follows.

Before posting a single piece of content, I focused on the infrastructure: what's our visual identity, how do we produce consistently, who does what, and how do we scale without burning people out? I treated this like a startup — documenting everything, building templates, and designing workflows that volunteers could actually sustain.

The brand identity came next — establishing a visual language that felt warm and contemporary, not institutional or generic. Only then did we start publishing, with a content calendar that balanced planned campaigns with room for spontaneous community moments.

What I Did

From nothing to a self-sustaining creative operation.

  • Built and launched the organization's primary Instagram channel from zero — establishing visual identity, content pillars, and tone of voice from scratch.
  • Designed and documented end-to-end content production workflows, enabling volunteer contributors to produce consistently without constant oversight.
  • Produced and art-directed 150+ multimedia assets including photography, graphics, Reels, and event coverage across a 12-month period.
  • Mentored 4 team members on content creation, editing, and social strategy — doubling the team's weekly output capacity within 3 months.
  • Built a content calendar system in Notion that balanced evergreen programming with real-time community storytelling.
  • Analyzed Instagram Insights weekly to iterate on post timing, format mix, and content themes — driving sustained algorithmic reach growth.

Results

A community that shows up — online and off.

Within the first year, the channel achieved 113% reach growth and a 35% month-over-month follower increase — entirely organic, with no paid amplification. The reach-to-follower ratio of 70× indicated strong content resonance well beyond the existing community.

More importantly: the program didn't depend on me. The workflows, templates, and team I built continued running after my active involvement scaled back — the truest measure of operational success.
